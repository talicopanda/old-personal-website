YouTube

  • 3Blue1Brown
    This is by far the best math YouTube channel out there. With great mathematical visualizations, Grant explains complex concepts intuitively and visualy appealing.

  • Kurzgesagt - In a Nutshell
    With beautiful animations and a team of scientists, Kurzgesagt tackles deep topics like String Theory or important issues in today's society.

  • Mark Rober
    You've probably already seen at least one of his videos (even if you don't know him). The former NASA employee does some insane projects and explains the physics behind them.

  • Computerphile
    Necessary channel for any computer science student. They cover pretty much all fundamental topics in the field.

  • Numberphile
    Impressively enough, this one is very similar to Computerphile except it deals more with math and numbers (wow... mindblown).

  • SmarterEveryDay
    From engineering to computer science, Destin produces amazing content with footage that will blow your mind.

  • Vsauce
    Michael is able to transform the weirdest topics into an interesting discussion with academic implications.

  • Veritasium
    This one is a bit more physics and chemistry related, but it is still a reliable source for amazing content.

  • The School of Life
    I needed one more to fill up the screen, but this one is still a great resource to keep up the mental sanity.
